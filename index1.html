About

Academic Profile

SB

Dr. Sarveshwar Bharti is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Guru Nanak Dev University (GNDU), Amritsar, one of India's prominent research universities. He holds a Doctor of Philosophy degree and brings deep expertise at the intersection of software engineering and modern data-driven techniques.

His scholarly work focuses on advancing the understanding of software development through data-driven approaches — particularly how sentiment analysis and machine learning can illuminate developer behaviour, code quality, and engineering team dynamics. His research on software clone management and developer sentiments has contributed meaningful insights to the software analytics community.

Beyond software engineering, Dr. Bharti has applied social media analytics and ML methodologies to domain challenges such as understanding stakeholder behaviour during the COVID-19 pandemic, demonstrating the interdisciplinary reach of his research program.

He maintains active profiles on major academic indexing platforms and has been recognised with a published patent, reflecting both the scholarly and applied dimensions of his contributions.

Research Interests

Areas of Expertise

Bridging theoretical foundations in software engineering with practical applications of machine learning, Dr. Bharti's research spans multiple interconnected domains that collectively advance how we build, analyse, and understand software systems.

01
Software Engineering

Investigating best practices, tooling, and analytical approaches to improve software quality, maintainability, and developer productivity. Particular focus on code cloning and its management within modern IDEs.

Code Clones IDE Tooling Systematic Reviews
02
Machine Learning

Applying classical and modern ML algorithms to real-world problems spanning software analytics and social behaviour modelling, with emphasis on practical, deployable models that drive actionable insights.

Classification Feature Engineering Predictive Models
03
Sentiment Analysis

Pioneering the application of NLP-driven sentiment mining to software engineering contexts — deciphering developer emotions from Stack Overflow, commit messages, and code review discussions.

NLP Emotion Mining Developer Behaviour
04
Data Science

Harnessing large-scale datasets — from software repositories to social media streams — to extract statistically grounded conclusions that inform engineering practice and public understanding.

Data Mining Statistical Analysis Visualisation
05
Software Analytics

Mining software repositories and project artefacts to understand how software evolves, how teams collaborate, and how historical patterns can predict future quality and risk.

MSR Repository Mining Process Analytics
06
Developers' Analytics

Studying developer psychology, work patterns, and communication to understand how human factors shape software quality — bridging the gap between computer science and organisational behaviour.

Human Factors Team Dynamics Productivity
